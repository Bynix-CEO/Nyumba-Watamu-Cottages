Canyon Marafa

Canyon Marafa, also known as "The Hell's Kitchen," is a stunning geological formation located in the coast of Kenya near Malindi. It's famous for its colorful cliffs and unique rock formations

Experience the awe-inspiring beauty of Canyon Marafa, where striking rock formations and vibrant hues create a surreal landscape. Explore this natural wonder and be mesmerized by its dramatic vistas and unique geological marvels.

Fun Activities on Canyon Marafa

  • Photography

  • Picnicking

Gede Ruins

Step back in time at the Gede Ruins, where ancient Swahili architecture and mysterious, overgrown ruins tell the story of a once-thriving city. Wander through these evocative remains and uncover the secrets of a lost civilization amidst lush tropical gardens

Gede’s prosperity peaked in the 15th century but by the 17th century the town had been abandoned. The town’s abandonment is attributed to several factors including; raids from neighboring communities, arrival of the Portuguese, conflict in neighboring city states of Malindi and Mombasa, and a drop in the water table. In 1929 it was declared a protected monument and in the late 1930’s some of the buildings which were in danger of collapsing were repaired by the Public Works Department of the Kenya Government. Sitting on 45 acres of land, excavations at the site continue to this day
